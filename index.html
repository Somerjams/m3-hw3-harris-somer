She is the oldest of three children to parents, Monica, and Ronald Fenty. Rihanna signed with Def Jam Records at age 16 and in 2005 released her first album Music of the Sun, which sold more than two million copies worldwide. After being discovered by music producer Evan Rodgers, he visited the island with his Barbadian wife. She moved to Connecticut to live with Rodgers and his wife, to cut a four-song demo. “When I left Barbados, I didn’t look back”, said Rihanna. I wanted to do what I had to do, even if it meant moving to America”. She went on to release more albums and an array of hit songs, including "Unfaithful," "Umbrella," "Disturbia," "Take a Bow," "Diamonds" and "We Found Love."
